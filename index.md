**How does social meaning interact with language change?**

I'm a PhD student and graduate assistant in Michigan State University's [**Department of Linguistics, Languages, and Cultures**](https://lilac.msu.edu/linguistics/) where I concentrate in sociolinguistics. I work with Betsy Sneller, Suzanne Evans Wagner,  Karthik Durvasula, and others to investigate questions related to sociophonetics/phonology, language change, and the linguistic construction of identity. I am highly involved in the MI Diaries project, a large-scale project conducted by the MSU Sociolinguistics Lab that has been remotely gathering acoustic data (through participant self-recording) from hundreds of Michiganders since early 2020.

I am especially interested in finding ways to marry concepts from third-wave sociolinguistics, linguistic anthropology, and sociocultural linguistics with experimental and mixed-methods approaches to address the fundamental questions of language change as laid out by Weinreich, Labov, and Herzog (1968). Specifically, I strive to investigate the degree and nature of interaction between the dynamics of language change and socioindexical meaning. I am additionally invested in exploring the extent to which these concepts can be integrated within a framework of cultural evolution to offer holistic explanations of the actuation and spread of linguistic change. 

Currently, I am working with Dr. Sneller on a review paper looking at the Labovian sociolinguistic interview and sociolinguistic prompts, while also introducing an analysis of the kinds of prompts we have found to be most successful in the MI Diaries Project. I am also conducting research for my first qualifying paper, in which I am using data from the MI Diaries Project to determine whether and how younger Michiganders, who are living in a situation of rapid sound change, are varying their pronunciation of particular vowels between conservative (Northern Cities Shift) and progressive (Low-Back-Merger Shift) variants when taking stances related to the social constructs of "childhood" and "teenager-hood."
